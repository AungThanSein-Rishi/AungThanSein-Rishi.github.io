Bank Churn Rate Analysis

Project Overview

This interactive dashboard provides a detailed view of customer churn behavior across demographics, financial standing, and product engagement. Built in Power BI, it integrates customer-level data to uncover key drivers of churn and retention. The solution enables banks to identify high-risk segments, analyze churn trends, and design proactive customer retention strategies.

Key Features

Churn & Customer Metrics

  • Total Customers Analyzed: 10,000
  • Overall Churn Rate: 20.4% (≈2,037 churned customers)
  • KPIs on: Average Age (39), Tenure (5 years), Avg. Bank Balance ($76K), and Avg. Credit Score (651).

Churn Insights by Dimension

  • Churn Status: 20.4% of customers churned, holding 24.3% of total bank balance ($186M).
  • Product Churn:
    • Product 1: Moderate churn rate (27.7%), majority of customers retained.
    • Product 2: Lowest churn rate (7.6%), strongest retention performance.
    • Product 3: High churn rate (82.7%), majority of customers lost.
    • Product 4: Complete churn (100%), all customers lost.
  • Gender: Female churn (25.1%) significantly higher than male churn (16.5%).
  • Geography: Germany records the highest churn (32.4%), vs. France (16.2%) and Spain (16.7%).

Financial Metrics Impact

  • Bank Balance: Customers with low balances (<$80K) churn more (22.1%), while very high balances (200K) also show churn (>21.6%).
  • Credit Score: Low scores (<580) show higher churn (22%), while excellent scores (≥800) show stability (19.5%).
  • Estimated Salary: Premium earners (>200K) still churn at 21.6%, highlighting competitive risks.

Customer Segments

  • Age:Younger (20–30) and new customers (<2 years tenure) show higher churn risk (up to 23%).
  • Tenure: Mid-tenure customers (5–8 years) are the most loyal.

Formula & Calculation

Churn metrics are derived using DAX measures to calculate churn %, retention rates, and customer segment breakdowns. Key measures include:

  • Churn Rate:Churned Customers ÷ Total Customers

Business Impact

  • Customer Retention Strategy: Clear identification of at-risk groups (females, Germans, low credit scores, premium earners).
  • Product Optimization: Product 3 & 4 require urgent review to prevent further loss.
  • Targeted Interventions: Early-tenure onboarding, female-focused campaigns, and premium customer white-glove services.
  • Financial Stability: Protecting $186M in balances held by churned customers is critical for long-term profitability.
